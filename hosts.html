Web Hosting

HostGator

HostGator’s plans start at $2.64 a month for a 3-year term, and their top tier plan is priced at $5.04 a month for a 3-year term. With all of their plans, HostGator throws in a free domain for a year. All of their plans, from their hatchling plan to their business plan, include unmetered bandwidth, domain registration, SSL certificates, malware installs, and one click WordPress installs. One good reason why you should choose HostGator is that they are trusted by professionals, with ⅓ of their customers being web pros.

BlueHost

Bluehost’s plans start at $1.99 for the first month, renewing at $7.99. Their top tier plan is priced at $9.99 for the first month, renewing at $20.99. All of Bluehost’s plans include tools to help you digitally grow your business, a domain manager, SSL certificates aimed to help securitize personal information and data, WordPress, which helps consumers build and manage their websites, and they list your business online through google searches, allowing customers easy access to important information regarding the business. Some of the reasons why you would choose Bluehost include simple WordPress setup, industry leading speeds, automatic updating of WordPress, website backups, and an array of website tools that allow you to meet your advanced website needs.

GoDaddy

GoDaddy’s web hosting service standard performance plans start at $5.99 a month for a 3-year term. Their top tier plan in this category is priced at $17.99 a month for a 3-year term. Their high performance web hosting service plans, which are recommended for multiple high traffic or resource heavy sites, start at $17.99 a month for a 3-year term. Their top tier plan in this category is priced at $54.99 for a 3-year term. All of Godaddy’s web hosting service plans include WordPress, easy-to-use control panels, unmetered bandwidth, automatic daily backups, and global data centers. Some of the reasons why you would choose Godaddy include fast loading pages, flexible resources, and 24/7 security.

My Recommendation

The web hosting site that I would recommend using is Bluehost. I would recommend BlueHost because of their relationship with WordPress, which they have been working with since 2005. Also they provide free SSL certificates, which help safeguard your personal and business information, on top of a free domain for a year. Out of these three web hosting sites, BlueHost is the most expensive, but their prices are not far off of GoDaddy’s, which I believe to be reasonable considering the reliability that BlueHost provides.
